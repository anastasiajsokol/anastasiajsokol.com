Welcome!

This is the page (or collection of pages) I am most excited about within the entire website! There are (currently) two main sections.

  • Algorithm Library

    Here you can find descriptions, implementations, and analyses of algorithms that I find particularly interesting. In general, as the name of the page would imply, this content is intended to be useful as a pedagogical resource. That being said, the exact focus placed on a given algorithm may or may not align with how a specific course may want to focus on it.

  • Special Topics

    While my primary focus is computer science — and probably will remain so into the future — some content does not quite fall within the scope of what could arguably be called an algorithm. Those topics will be put onto the special topics page.

  • Courses

    As I learn more about designing courses I will start creating some course content — and eventually entire courses — then putting that content under the courses page. For now no course is anywhere near complete enough to be worth uploading, so this page is inactive. I do not recommend using content placed there at face value. I currently do not have a degree in education.

Usage, Linking, and Citation

Check back later for my guidelines. Generally, you are okay to do whatever you want, but there may be some specific restrictions in the future, and maybe more importantly information on how to link to content depending on your use case.
